Dr. Alan Jackson

Co-Investigator

  Dr Jackson is an astronomer and planetary scientist whose research focuses on understanding the formation and evolution of planetary systems, and how differences in formation processes lead to differences between our Solar System and exoplanetary systems. He is particularly interested in the final stages of the assembly of terrestrial planets. He is contributing to Task 3 by conducting Smoothed-Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) simulations of giant impacts between planetary embryos, which he will use to quantify how giant impacts change mantle and bulk-planet elemental abundances. He will also contribute to Task 2, examining how rates of pebble accretion are influenced by orbital eccentricity.
