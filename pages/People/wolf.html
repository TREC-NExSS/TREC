Dr. Aaron Wolf

Co-Investigator

  Aaron Wolf (Co-Investigator) is a planetary scientist who uses computational modeling to understand how silicate melts drive rocky planet formation and evolution. This includes their role in both the formation of planetary interiors—through core-formation and magma ocean evolution—as well as the compositional evolution of planetary surfaces—through volcanism and outgassing. He approaches this topic using a variety of computational techniques, including thermodynamic model building, thermo- and geo-dynamic simulation, atomistic simulation, and statistical analysis. He is participating in tasks 3 and 4 of the TREC project by extending the popular magma modeling software MELTS to enable integrated thermodynamic and geodynamic modeling of magma ocean crystallization, crustal formation, and volcanic outgassing for the range of rocky planets found in and outside our solar system. All modeling tools developed for TREC are also being released as open-source software as part of his lead developer role at the ENKI project.
