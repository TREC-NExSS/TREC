Prof. Steven Desch

Principal Investigator

  Steve Desch (Principal Investigator) is a theoretical astrophysicist specializing in combining meteoritic data with astrophysical modeling, to determine the history of our solar system and to improve models of (exo)planet formation. He is particularly interested in the problems of chondrule formation, the origins of short-lived radionuclides, the distribution of CAIs in the solar nebula, and condensation fronts in protoplanetary disks. He also works on models of the origin and evolution of Kuiper Belt Objects. He is leading the effort in Task 2 to model redistribution of elements within the protoplanetary disk due to pebble drift and snow lines, and contributing to Task 1 to model chemical fractionations during molecular cloud core collapse.
